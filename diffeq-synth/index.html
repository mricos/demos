Models

The simplest resonant system. A mass on a spring with friction. The fundamental building block of all acoustic resonance.

Parameters: Freq sets the natural frequency, Damping controls energy loss rate, Drive applies a sinusoidal forcing function.

A self-oscillating nonlinear system. Originally modeled vacuum tube circuits. Produces limit cycles — the oscillation amplitude self-regulates.

μ controls nonlinearity strength. Low μ = nearly sinusoidal. High μ = relaxation oscillations (sharp, buzzy).

A nonlinear spring with cubic stiffness. Can exhibit chaotic behavior, period doubling, and strange attractors. Rich, unpredictable timbres.

α = linear stiffness, β = cubic stiffness (negative = softening spring), δ = damping, γ = driving amplitude.

Two damped harmonic oscillators connected by a spring. Models sympathetic resonance, beating, energy transfer between modes.

Coupling k controls the connection strength. Freq 2 and Damp 2 set the second oscillator's properties.

Visualizations

  • Phase Portrait — position vs velocity. Spirals = damped, circles = undamped, limit cycles = Van der Pol
  • Waveform — time-domain signal colored by instantaneous energy
  • Energy — kinetic + potential over time, colored by damping regime
  • Spectrum — FFT magnitude, shows harmonics and nonlinear distortion

Color Mapping

Colors encode physics: hue = energy level (blue=low, red=high), saturation = damping ratio, brightness = velocity.

Damped Harmonic Oscillator

\ddot{x} + 2\zeta\omega_n\dot{x} + \omega_n^2 x = F(t)
ωn natural frequency = 2πf
ζ damping ratio (0=none, 1=critical)
F(t) driving force = A·sin(ωd·t)

Van der Pol

\ddot{x} - \mu(1 - x^2)\dot{x} + \omega_n^2 x = 0
μ nonlinearity parameter

When μ > 0, energy is pumped in at small amplitudes and dissipated at large amplitudes, creating a stable limit cycle.

Duffing Equation

\ddot{x} + \delta\dot{x} + \alpha x + \beta x^3 = \gamma\cos(\omega t)
α linear stiffness
β cubic stiffness (nonlinear term)
δ damping coefficient
γ forcing amplitude

Coupled System

\begin{aligned} \ddot{x}_1 + 2\zeta_1\omega_1\dot{x}_1 + \omega_1^2 x_1 + k(x_1 - x_2) &= F(t) \\ \ddot{x}_2 + 2\zeta_2\omega_2\dot{x}_2 + \omega_2^2 x_2 + k(x_2 - x_1) &= 0 \end{aligned}
k coupling spring constant

Energy

E = \tfrac{1}{2}\dot{x}^2 + \tfrac{1}{2}\omega_n^2 x^2

Total mechanical energy: kinetic + potential. In damped systems, E decreases monotonically. In driven systems, E oscillates around a steady state.

RK4 Integration

y_{n+1} = y_n + \tfrac{h}{6}(k_1 + 2k_2 + 2k_3 + k_4)

Fourth-order Runge-Kutta: the ODE solver running at audio sample rate. Stable and accurate for stiff oscillatory systems.
