▼ Twistor Fundamentals & Two-Component Spinors

Twistors are fundamental geometric objects that encode both the spin and momentum of massless particles. They live in a complex 4-dimensional space and provide the foundation for understanding two-component spinor geometry.

The Fundamental Spaces

Penrose's theory establishes correspondences between:

  • S: Two-component spinor space (SL(2,ℂ))
  • M: Complexified Minkowski spacetime
  • PT: Projective twistor space ℂP³

Two-Component Spinor Structure

The two-component spinors split into:

  • ψ_A: Undotted spinor (positive helicity, +ω frequency)
  • ψ'_A': Dotted spinor (negative helicity, -ω frequency)

This frequency splitting ±ω is fundamental to quantum field theory, where positive frequencies correspond to particles and negative frequencies to antiparticles.

▼ Clifford-Hopf Fibering & Geometric Connections

The Clifford-Hopf fibering provides the crucial geometric bridge connecting spinor space S to projective twistor space PT. This fibration reveals how rotational dynamics in real spacetime correspond to holomorphic transformations in twistor space.

The Null Flag Geometry

Each point in complexified spacetime M corresponds to a line in projective twistor space PT. This correspondence is mediated by the null flag structure of two-component spinors:

  • α-planes: Spanned by undotted spinors ψ_A
  • β-planes: Spanned by dotted spinors ψ'_A'

Bi-twistors and Split-Octonions

The geometric structure extends to bi-twistors and connects to split-octonion geometry, revealing deeper algebraic relationships in the foundations of spacetime geometry.

▼ The Penrose Transform & Physical Interpretation

The Penrose transform provides the ultimate connection between the holomorphic world of twistors and the real geometry of spacetime. It shows how twistor functions map to physical fields in spacetime.

The Complete Picture

What emerges is a unified framework where:

  • Quantum mechanics: Encoded in spinor frequency decomposition ±ω
  • General relativity: Emerges from spacetime-twistor correspondence
  • Complex analysis: Drives the holomorphic structure of PT

Visualizing the Theory

  • Rotation axes: Correspond to spinor field orientations
