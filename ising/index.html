Overview

Imagine trying to remember a name at a party. Someone mentions a detail and suddenly the name clicks into place — the mind settling from a messy hint into a clear memory. A Hopfield network models exactly this.

At its center is a quantity called energy — not the energy that powers a light bulb, but a carefully engineered bookkeeping device. Think of it as a score or altitude on a landscape whose job is to guarantee the network’s activity moves in a reliable direction.

The surprise is that it looks so much like real physical energy (the Ising Hamiltonian) that it’s tempting to treat them as the same thing. The real insight is understanding why they’re similar, why they’re different, and why the resemblance is useful.

Temperature (T)

Temperature controls the balance between order and exploration.

At low T, the system is greedy — almost never accepts a move that raises energy. Spins freeze into large ordered domains. At high T, thermal noise dominates and spins flip randomly.

The critical temperature Tc ≈ 2.269 is the phase transition: below it, spontaneous magnetization; above, disorder.

Physics Connection

In Boltzmann machines, temperature is a control knob for exploration. High T lets the network escape shallow valleys; low T forces it to commit. This is simulated annealing.

Try

  • T = 0.5 + Run — domains freeze
  • T = 4.0 — random static
  • T ≈ 2.27 — critical fluctuations
Grid Size (N)

Sets the lattice to N×N spins. Larger grids show sharper phase transitions but run slower.

N=8: fluctuations dominate. N=64: clear domain walls, dramatic transition near Tc.

Finite-Size Effects

The true phase transition only occurs in an infinite lattice. On finite grids, the transition is rounded. Larger N approaches the sharp ideal.

Coupling Constant (J)

J is the connection strength between neighbors. It determines what the system wants.

J > 0 (ferro): aligned neighbors lower E. System wants uniform regions.

J < 0 (antiferro): opposite neighbors lower E. System wants a checkerboard.

J = 0: no coupling. Spins independent.

Hopfield Analogy

In Hopfield, Jij → learned weights wij. Positive = agree. Negative = disagree. The pattern of agreements encodes memory.

Speed (Sweeps/Frame)

Monte Carlo sweeps per animation frame. One sweep = N² random spin-flip attempts.

Low (1): watch domain dynamics. High (10-20): reach equilibrium quickly.

Run / Pause Space

Starts or pauses continuous Metropolis simulation. Each frame runs Speed sweeps.

The Metropolis-Hastings algorithm: pick random spin, compute ΔE, accept if ΔE ≤ 0 or with probability exp(-ΔE/T).

Step S

One Monte Carlo sweep (N² flip attempts), then pause. Watch evolution step-by-step.

Reset R

Stops simulation, re-randomizes lattice, clears history.

Initial State

All Up: every spin = +1. Minimum energy for J>0.

All Down: every spin = -1. Same energy (symmetry).

Random: equal probability +1/-1. Maximally disordered.

Why It Matters

Ordered start at high T → watch disordering. Random start at low T → watch ordering, like Hopfield recalling from a noisy cue.

Temperature Sweep

Sharp drop near Tc ≈ 2.269 = second-order phase transition.

Energy (E/N²)

Energy per spin. Measures how well the network’s “desires” are satisfied.

For J=1: min = -2.0 (perfect alignment), max = +2.0.

Not “Real” Energy

Hopfield energy doesn’t power a toaster — it’s a scoring function predicting where the system settles. Like a “stress number” for a group of friends.

Magnetization (M)

M = (1/N²) Σ si. Ranges -1 to +1. Near zero = disorder.

The order parameter. In Hopfield, it measures how strongly the network has committed to a memory.

Current Temperature

Mirrors the slider. Tc = 2/ln(1+√2) ≈ 2.269, Onsager’s exact result (1944).

Sweep Count

Monte Carlo sweeps since reset. ~100 sweeps typically reaches equilibrium.

Hopfield Network

Memory isn’t in a single neuron but in the connections — patterns reconstruct from partial cues. A synthetic magnet made of neuron-like bits.

Energy Landscape

Every on/off pattern = a location on a landscape. Updates go downhill. The final valley = the recalled memory. Energy measures internal consistency.

Physics vs Hopfield

Physical energy: conserved, measured in joules. Hopfield energy: a designed scoring function. Break symmetry and it may not exist. Yet the resemblance gives us powerful intuitions: landscapes, valleys, attractors.

Spurious Memories

Extra valleys form between stored patterns. Energy guarantees settling, not perfection — a local minimum, not the global one.

Drawing Patterns

Click and drag to paint cells. Blue = +1, dark = -1. Or use the preset buttons: L, T, X, smiley.

Memories are spread across all connections. Your drawing defines the co-activation pattern that becomes a memory.

Store (Hebbian Learning)

“Cells that fire together wire together.”

Both on or both off → weight increases (agree). One on, one off → weight decreases (disagree). Each Store adds a valley to the energy landscape.

Capacity

10×10 grid stores ~14 patterns (0.14N). Beyond that: spurious states, blended memories.

Add Noise

Flips 30% of cells randomly — a partial cue. The noisy pattern is on the slope of a valley, not the bottom.

Recall (Animated)

Iterates asynchronously, one frame per iteration, so you can watch the pattern heal:

  1. hi = Σj wij sj
  2. si = sign(hi)
  3. Repeat until stable

Each step lowers energy. Guaranteed by symmetric weights (Lyapunov function). The network slides into the nearest valley.

Analogy

The update rule is gravity; energy is height. It doesn’t power the network — it explains its direction.

Clear

Resets grid, erases all stored patterns and weights.
